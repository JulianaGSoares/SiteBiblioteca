Books Available:

» Dom Casmurro

Dom Casmurro by Machado de Assis is a classic Brazilian novel that explores themes of love, jealousy, and memory. Narrated by Bento Santiago, known as Dom Casmurro, it tells the story of his obsessive love for Capitu, his childhood friend and wife, and his suspicions of her infidelity. The novel’s ambiguous narrative leaves readers questioning whether Capitu was guilty or if Bento’s jealousy distorted his reality.

» A Hora da Estrela

A Hora da Estrela (The Hour of the Star) by Clarice Lispector is a poignant and reflective story about Macabéa, a poor and unremarkable typist living in Rio de Janeiro. Narrated by the philosophical and introspective writer Rodrigo S.M., the novel delves into themes of poverty, identity, and the human condition, capturing Macabéa's tragic existence with a mix of empathy and detachment.

» Memórias Póstumas de Brás Cubas

Memórias Póstumas de Brás Cubas (The Posthumous Memoirs of Brás Cubas), also by Machado de Assis, is a groundbreaking work of Brazilian literature. Narrated by Brás Cubas, a wealthy man who writes his memoirs after his death, the novel uses wit, irony, and philosophical musings to critique society, human ambition, and morality. It is considered a precursor to modernist literature due to its innovative narrative style.

» O Cortiço

O Cortiço (The Slum) by Aluísio Azevedo is a naturalist novel that examines life in a crowded tenement in Rio de Janeiro. Through the lives of its diverse inhabitants, the novel explores themes of poverty, class, social determinism, and human instincts. Azevedo provides a vivid and critical portrayal of urban life and the struggles of Brazil’s lower classes in the late 19th century.
